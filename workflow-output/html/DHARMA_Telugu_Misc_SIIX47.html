Craftsmanship of Vēḷuguṁṇṭha Ācāṟlu

1śrī vēḷuguṁṇṭha Ācāṟlu
2paṇi kēsiri

Translation by Sastri (1969)

Śrī Vēḷuguṇṭha ācārlu did the work.

Translation by Jens Thomas

Śrī Vēḷuguṁṇṭha Ācāṟlu did the work.

Commentary

According to the information about the find spot Vēḷuguṁṇṭha Ācāṟlu was entrusted with work regarding the seventh cave temple at Bhairavakoṇḍa (Bhairavakōna). The form kēsiri is conservative in that it preserved initial k- instead of c- and is thus possibly indicative of old age. Vēḷuguṁṇṭha is a place name (’thousand ponds’), ācāṟlu is the Telugu plural of the ultimately skt. loanword ācārya and denotes a craftsman. Confer the similar expression pallavācārjya- (Master 1956: 24–25) that contains the name of the dynasty as its first member.

Bibliography

The inscription was noted in A. R. No. 272 of 1922 and first published by J. Ramayya Pantulu (1948: 18, № 47). K. M. Sastri re-edited the inscription in normalized spelling and provided a translation (1969: 283, № 3).
