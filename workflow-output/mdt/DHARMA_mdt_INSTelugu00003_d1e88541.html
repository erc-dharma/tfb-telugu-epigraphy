Remarks: "Only the estampage of the first side was published. In regard to side B the editors say that ""[t]he following is engraved on another piece probably as imprecatory portion"" (Ramesh & Ramachandra Murthy 1969-1970: 158, an. 7). ARIE attributes this inscription to the Bāṇa dynasty. However, the inscription mentions Davaḷeyarāja who could be identical with Pāṇḍuraṅga's son Niravadya-Dhavala mentioned in the Maliyapūṇḍi grant of Amma II (Calukya). The date interval reflects the rule of Vijayāditya III under whose reign Paṇḍaraṅga served. However, Davaḷeyrtāja could also have served under the rule of Bhīma I. The inscriptions refers to the donation of a pannasa but it is not clear whether it is a religious or secular land grant."
